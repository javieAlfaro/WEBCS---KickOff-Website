HISTORY.

Football, dating back to ancient civilizations, evolved over centuries into its modern form. In 1863, the Football Association (FA) in England established formal rules, separating it from rugby. The sport grew internationally, with FIFA founded in 1904, and the first World Cup held in 1930. Over time, football became the world's most popular sport, with major tournaments like the World Cup and UEFA Champions League solidifying its global appeal.

The sport has deep roots, with early versions played in ancient China, Greece, and Rome, but its modern form began in 19th-century England. In 1863, the Football Association (FA) was formed, creating the first standardized rules and separating it from rugby. The sport quickly spread across Europe and beyond, leading to the formation of FIFA in 1904 to oversee international competitions. The first FIFA World Cup took place in 1930 in Uruguay, cementing football as a global sport. Over the decades, football's popularity soared, with leagues and tournaments worldwide, including the UEFA Champions League, World Cup, and the rise of women's football with the first Women's World Cup in 1991. Today, football is the most-watched and played sport globally, uniting cultures and nations through the beautiful game.
