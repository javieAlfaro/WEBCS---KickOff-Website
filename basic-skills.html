Skills

Dribbling

Controlling and maneuvering the ball while moving.

  • Close Control: Keeping the ball close with light touches.
  • Speed Dribbling: Accelerating past opponents with quick, short touches.
  • Change of Direction: Using moves like step-overs or feints to evade defenders.

Passing

Moving the ball to a teammate with various techniques.

  • Short Passes: Using the inside of the foot for accurate, close-range passes.
  • Long Passes: Using the instep for powerful, long-distance passes.
  • One-Touch Passes: Passing with a single touch to keep the play flowing.

Shooting

Striking the ball towards the goal to score.

  • Power Shots: Using the laces for strong shots.
  • Placement Shots: Using the inside of the foot for accurate placement.
  • Volley: Hitting the ball mid-air before it touches the ground.

Defending

Preventing the opponent from scoring through various techniques.

  • Tackling: Winning the ball through controlled challenges.
  • Marking: Staying close to limit an opponent’s movement.
  • Blocking: Using the body to block shots or passes.

Heading

Using the forehead to direct the ball for defense, passing, or scoring.

  • Defensive Heading: Clearing the ball from the defensive area.
  • Attacking Heading: Directing the ball towards the goal from set pieces or crosses.

Fun Facts. Did you know?

The average professional football player can run up to 10 miles in a single game.

The current world record for longest goal in a competitive football match is 96 meters and was set by British goalkeeper Tom King.

The game as we know it began in England in the 19th century, despite history showing games played with a ball and feet dating back over 2,000 years.
