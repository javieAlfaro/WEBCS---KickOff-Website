Objectives

Score More Goals Than the Opponent:

The main goal of football is to score more goals than the opposing team within the allocated time (usually 90 minutes, divided into two halves of 45 minutes each). The team with the most goals at the end of the match is declared the winner. If both teams score the same number of goals, the match is a draw.

Maintain Possession and Control:

Ball Control - Teams aim to maintain possession of the ball to control the game, create scoring opportunities, and minimize the opponent’s chances of scoring.

Passing and Movement - Effective passing and movement are crucial for maintaining possession and building up plays to reach the opponent’s goal.

Defend Against the Opponent:

Defensive Strategies - Teams must also focus on defending their own goal by preventing the opponent from scoring. This involves positioning, blocking shots, and tackling.

Goalkeeper’s Role - The goalkeeper plays a key role in defending the goal, using their hands and body to save shots on target.
